Veggie Omelette + Avocado Toast + Orange
🥚🥚🥚🥬🍅🧅🥑🍞🍊
🔥 390 kcal💪 24g protein 🌾 28g carbs🥑 18g fat 🌿 7g fibre
🛒 Ingredients
IngredientQuantityPrepkcal
Large eggs3 wholewhisked210
Baby spinach40g (2 big handfuls)washed9
Cherry tomatoes60g (5–6 tomatoes)halved11
Onion30g (¼ small onion)finely diced12
Avocado (ripe)75g (½ medium avocado)mashed120
Whole-grain bread1 slice (35g)toasted85
Orange1 medium (150g)peeled62
Olive oil spray1 spray (≈2g)—8
Salt, black pepperpinch each—0
TOTAL~390
👨‍🍳 Method
  1. Spray a non-stick pan lightly with olive oil. Heat over medium. Add diced onion and cook 2 min until soft.
  2. Add halved cherry tomatoes and spinach. Stir 1 min until spinach just wilts. Push to side of pan.
  3. Pour whisked eggs over vegetables. Season with salt and pepper. Cook undisturbed 2 min until edges set.
  4. Fold omelette in half. Cook 1 more min. Slide onto plate.
  5. Toast bread. Mash avocado with a fork, season with salt and a squeeze of lemon if desired. Spread on toast.
  6. Serve omelette + toast alongside peeled orange. Eat immediately.
💡 Liver Tip: Eggs provide choline which is essential for liver fat transport. Avocado provides glutathione — a powerful liver antioxidant. Take your Vit D3 + Acetyl L-Carnitine now with this meal.

👨‍🍳 Method

Shrimp Stir-Fry on Cauliflower Rice

👨‍🍳 Method

Poached Eggs + Mushroom Spinach Sauté + Rye Toast
🥚🥚🍄🥬🍊🍞
🔥 350 kcal💪 20g protein 🌾 30g carbs🥑 13g fat 🌿 6g fibre
🛒 Ingredients
IngredientQuantityPrepkcal
Large eggs2 wholeat room temperature140
Brown/cremini mushrooms120g (1 cup sliced)sliced26
Baby spinach60gwashed14
Rye bread1 slice (35g)toasted83
Grapefruit½ medium (120g flesh)segmented39
Olive oil1 tsp (5ml)—40
Garlic clove1 small (3g)minced4
White vinegar1 tbsp (for poaching)—2
Fresh thyme (optional)1 sprigleaves stripped1
Salt, black pepperpinch each—0
TOTAL~350
👨‍🍳 Method
  1. Heat olive oil in a pan over medium-high. Add mushrooms in a single layer — do NOT stir for 2 min so they brown.
  2. Add garlic and thyme. Stir and cook 1 more min. Add spinach, toss until wilted (1 min). Season. Keep warm.
  3. Toast rye bread. Bring 8cm of water to a gentle simmer in a deep saucepan. Add vinegar.
  4. Crack each egg into a small cup. Create a gentle whirlpool in the water. Slide egg in. Cook 3 min for soft yolk, 4 min for firm.
  5. Remove eggs with a slotted spoon. Pat dry on paper towel. Season with pepper.
  6. Plate: rye toast, mushroom-spinach mix, eggs on top. Grapefruit on the side. Take Vit D3 + Acetyl L-Carnitine.
💡 Tip: Mushrooms are rich in selenium and B vitamins that support liver detoxification enzymes. Don't wash them — wipe with a damp cloth so they sear, not steam.

🌅Breakfast
Baked Avocado Egg Boats + Watermelon
🥑🥚🥚🌿🧀🍉
🔥 370 kcal💪 18g protein 🌾 22g carbs🥑 22g fat 🌿 7g fibre
🛒 Ingredients
IngredientQuantityPrepkcal
Ripe avocado1 medium (150g whole)halved, stone removed160
Small eggs2—140
Cherry tomatoes40g (3–4)quartered, for topping7
Fresh chives or coriander5gfinely chopped2
Chilli flakespinch (optional)—1
Lemon juice1 tspto prevent browning1
Watermelon200g (1 cup cubed)—60
Salt, black pepperpinch each—0
TOTAL~370
👨‍🍳 Method
  1. Preheat oven to 220°C/425°F. Line a small baking tray with foil.
  2. Cut avocado in half, remove stone. Scoop out a little extra flesh to make the hollow larger (eat it separately or save for later). Squeeze lemon over cut surface.
  3. Place avocado halves cut-side up in a muffin tin or prop upright with foil so they don't roll. Season hollows with salt and pepper.
  4. Carefully crack 1 small egg into each avocado hollow. If egg is too large, remove some white first.
  5. Bake 12–15 min for runny yolk, 15–18 min for fully set. Watch carefully — oven temperatures vary.
  6. Top with cherry tomatoes, chives and chilli flakes. Serve with watermelon. Take Vit D3 + Acetyl L-Carnitine.
💡 Tip: Use SMALL eggs — large eggs overflow. The avocado provides oleic acid (same as olive oil) which reduces liver inflammation. This is the most instagrammable breakfast of the week! 📸

Tofu & Veggie Buddha Bowl with Sesame-Ginger Dressing

👨‍🍳 Method
  1. Preheat oven 200°C/400°F. Toss sweet potato cubes with olive oil spray and pinch of salt. Roast 20–25 min, flipping halfway.
  2. Press and cube tofu. Toss with 1 tsp soy sauce. Add to roasting tray last 10 min of sweet potato cooking — tofu edges will crisp up.

💡 Tip: The secret to crispy baked tofu is pressing it well AND using only a light coating of soy sauce (not oil) — it creates a lacquered, chewy exterior. Edamame has 11g protein per 80g!

Scallop & Shrimp Seafood Salad + Whole-Grain Roll

👨‍🍳 Method
  1. Make lemon vinaigrette: whisk 1 tsp olive oil, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, salt and pepper. Set aside.

💡 Tip: Using the same pan for both shrimp and scallops builds flavour through the fond (brown bits). Deglaze with a splash of white wine or lemon juice to lift those flavours into the dish.

Smoked Salmon Eggs + Cucumber + Toast + Berries
🐟🥚🥚🥒🍞🫐
🔥 400 kcal💪 30g protein 🌾 24g carbs🥑 16g fat 🌿 4g fibre
🛒 Ingredients
IngredientQuantityPrepkcal
Smoked salmon (slices)80gready to eat105
Large eggs2 wholefor scrambling140
Whole-grain bread1 slice (35g)toasted85
Cucumber80g (½ small)thinly sliced rounds11
Mixed fresh berries100g—35
Low-fat cream cheese (optional)15g (1 tbsp)for toast24
Capers (optional)5g (1 tsp)rinsed1
Fresh dill5g—2
Olive oil spray / unsalted butter½ tspfor scrambling eggs17
Lemonfew dropsover salmon1
TOTAL~400
👨‍🍳 Method
  1. Whisk 2 eggs with a pinch of salt and pepper. Set aside.
  2. Toast bread. Spread with cream cheese if using.
  3. Heat ½ tsp butter in a non-stick pan over LOW heat (low and slow = creamy scrambled eggs). Add eggs.
  4. Gently push eggs with a spatula every 30 seconds, folding them — do NOT scramble vigorously. Remove from heat while still slightly wet-looking (residual heat will finish cooking).
  5. Layer cucumber slices on toast. Add smoked salmon. Squeeze lemon. Top with capers and dill.
  6. Serve scrambled eggs alongside toast + salmon. Berries on the side. Take Vit D3 + Acetyl L-Carnitine.
💡 Tip: Smoked salmon is rich in omega-3s AND astaxanthin — a powerful antioxidant that specifically targets liver inflammation. Low and slow for scrambled eggs = the French technique for the creamiest results.

Grilled Paneer & Veggie Skewers + Tzatziki + Potato Wedges

🛒 Ingredients
IngredientQuantityPrepkcal
Paneer120g2cm cubes300
Zucchini80gthick rounds14
Cherry tomatoes60g (5–6)whole11
Red onion60g (½ small)wedges24
Baby potatoes100ghalved77
Low-fat Greek yogurt (tzatziki)80g—48
Cucumber (tzatziki)40ggrated, squeezed dry5
Garlic clove (tzatziki)½ cloveminced or grated2
Dill / mint (tzatziki)5gchopped1
Olive oil1 tsp (5ml)for marinade40
Lemon juice + zest½ lemon—8
Paprika + oregano½ tsp eachfor marinade4
TOTAL~410
👨‍🍳 Method
  1. Make tzatziki: grate cucumber, squeeze out ALL excess water in a clean cloth. Mix with Greek yogurt, garlic, dill, pinch of salt. Refrigerate.
  2. Marinate paneer and vegetables: mix olive oil, lemon juice, paprika, oregano and salt. Toss paneer, zucchini, tomatoes and onion in marinade. Rest 20 min minimum.
  3. Roast potato wedges: toss with pinch of salt and olive oil spray. Bake at 200°C/400°F for 25 min until golden.
  4. Thread paneer and vegetables alternately onto skewers (soak wooden skewers 20 min first to prevent burning).
  5. Grill skewers on high heat (BBQ, grill pan or oven grill) 3–4 min per side until paneer is charred and vegetables are slightly soft.
  6. Serve skewers with potato wedges and tzatziki. Take Magnesium after dinner.
💡 Tip: Squeeze every last drop of water from the cucumber before making tzatziki — otherwise it will become watery within 30 min. Greek yogurt provides probiotics which support gut-liver axis health.
